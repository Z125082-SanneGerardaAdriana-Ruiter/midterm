    What I have learned so far

    During my stay in Japan, I’ve already learned a lot about Japanese culture. Although I don’t personally believe in any god, I was inspired by Shintoism, one of the two main religions in Japan. Shintoism and Buddhism have coexisted for over 1500 years and continue to shape Japanese traditions and daily life. To give a quick overview, I’ll highlight some of the key aspects of both Shintoism and Buddhism.

    Shintoism

    Indigenous religion in Japan that focuses on nature, spirits (kami), and ancestor worship. It has no founder, sacred texts or strict doctrines. Practices include purification, offerings and festivals. Shrines (jinja) are Shinto places of worship, emphasizing harmony with nature and life.

    Steps to pray

    1. Bow once in front of tori gate
    2. Trow a coin in the offerings box (5 yen)
    3. Bow deeply "twice"
    4. Clap your hands "twice"
    5. Pray in silence
    6. Bow one more time (also when passing tori gate again)

    Tip: dont walk trough the middle of the tori gate since it is meant for the Kami(gods)

    Buddhism

    Foreign religion from India that focuses on enlightenment, karma, and escaping the cycle of rebirth. It has scriptures, rituals and many sects. Practices include meditation, chanting and ancestor worship. Temples (tera) are Buddhist places of worship, emphasizing reflection, impermanence, and inner peace.

    Steps to pray

    1. Trow a coun in the offer box
    2. bow gently with your hands together (gasshō)
    3. close your eyes and pray in silence
    4. gently bow one more time

    Tip: Try to stay respectful when visiting tempels, people come there to pray for their religion. Dont see it as a tourist attraction.

    How to behave in Japan

    As someone who always tries to respect other cultures, the first few weeks in Japan were really challenging for me. I wasn’t used to the Japanese way of living and was constantly afraid of accidentally breaking social rules. But I learned that as long as you’re respectful and make an effort to follow local customs, you’ll be just fine. You don’t need to stress as much as I did—just be open and willing to learn.

    Do's

    • Take you shoes off
    • Try to sort trash
    • Try to speak Japanese
    • Respect local customs
    • Taste the local cuisine
    • You are allowed to pick up a noodles or small plates when eating

    Dont's

    • Destroy or touch tori gates
    • skip lines
    • Dont blow your nose in public
    • Trow your trash on the streat
    • dont stick your chopsticks in you rice
    • Dont bombard the geishas and maiko for pictures
